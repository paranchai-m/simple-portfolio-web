Marked Ericales diversity in late Oligocene–Early Miocene palynofloras from northern Thailand suggests stratified mountain forests

Journal of Systematics and Evolution (2025)

Objective: To investigate fossil Ericales pollen with a high taxonomic resolution from the Ban Pa Kha Subbasin and assess their palaeoenvironmental significance by comparing them with modern analogues and climate profiles.

Summary: This study identified 24 different ericalean pollen types, suggesting that the rich ensemble of Ericales in northern Thailand has existed since at least the late Oligocene. The findings indicate that the assemblage likely derives from stratified mountain forests with multiple vertical vegetation zones.

Community assembly of tropical Fagaceae-dominated forests in Thailand dates back at least to the Late Palaeogene

Botanical Journal of the Linnean Society (2023)

Objective: To investigate fossil pollen grains of Fagaceae from the Ban Pa Kha Subbasin using combined light microscopy (LM) and scanning electron microscopy (SEM) to obtain high taxonomic resolution and infer the palaeoenvironment of the region during the Late Oligocene to Early Miocene.

Summary: This study identified 15 fossil species of Fagaceae, suggesting that the rich ensemble of oak-dominated forests typical of Southeast Asia has existed since the Eocene, contradicting previous theories of a much later establishment.

Morphological and harmomegathic characters of pollen of Gnetum species (Gnetaceae) in Thailand

Botanical Journal of the Linnean Society (2018)

Objective: To study the pollen morphology and harmomegathic mechanisms (volume changes due to hydration/dehydration) of Gnetum species in Thailand using combined LM and SEM.

Summary: This research provides detailed descriptions of Gnetum pollen and explores how their unique morphological features relate to their physiological responses and evolutionary history.
